TASK 1: The Ultimate Developer ID Badge

This week, you aren't just putting text on a screen. You must design a complete, structured Developer ID Badge. It will test your ability to configure your project, nest layouts, and handle errors.

REQUIREMENTS (Read Carefully!):
1. THE DOCTOR: Run `flutter doctor` in your terminal. You must have NO ERRors for your target platform (Android or Chrome). Take a screenshot of the output.
2. THE CLEANUP: Create a new Flutter project (`flutter create my_id`). Delete EVERYTHING inside `main.dart` and start from scratch.
3. THE ASSET: You MUST use a LOCAL image for your profile picture (`AssetImage`), not a web URL. 
   - *Challenge*: To do this, you must create an `assets` folder, add a picture (like `profile.png`), and register it in `pubspec.yaml`. If your app says "Unable to load asset", check your spacing in `pubspec.yaml`! It must be exactly 2 spaces nested.
4. THE LAYOUT: 
   - A Scaffold with a dark background color.
   - A centralized Container (the ID Card itself) with rounded corners and a lighter background.
   - Inside the card: 
     - A top Row containing your local profile image (circular) and your Name next to it.
     - A Divider line underneath.
     - A Column below the divider containing your details (Title, Phone, Email) with matching icons next to them (Use Row inside Column!).
5. NO OVERFLOWS: If you get a "yellow and black warning tape" error on your screen, your widgets are too big for the screen. Fix it.

SUBMISSION:
Replace `screenshot.jpg` in this folder with a picture of your finished ID badge running on an emulator or real device. If you couldn't figure out the local image, use a placeholder icon, but you will lose points.
